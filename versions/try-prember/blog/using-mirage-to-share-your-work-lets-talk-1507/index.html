Using Mirage to share your work? Let's talk!

One of the best uses for Mirage is to demo your application.

Whether you're showing off a new feature to your coworkers or sharing an interactive prototype with potential customers, a Mirage-backed Ember app is a great way to get fast feedback on whatever you're building.

I've been talking to some folks using Mirage in this way, and while they love it, some consistent pain points keep coming up in my conversations:

  1. Losing data on page refreshes is a bummer. Demonstrating write operations can be confusing, given that those operations don't persist on page reload.

  2. It can be hard to share. Sometimes folks have to call colleagues over to their desk or use screen-sharing software to show off their work with ember s.

  3. It can be slow. When seeding Mirage with larger graphs of data, it can really slow down the initial render of the app.

  4. The data is unrealistic. It's hard to make realistic-looking data, either with Faker.js or custom seeding code.

  5. I wish I could toggle between multiple scenarios.

I've been brainstorming some solutions to these problems and I'm looking to get more input from developers.

If you or your team are using Mirage in this way and are running into any of these issues, I'd love to hear from you! Leave a reply here, DM me on Twitter, or email me at sam@embermap.com.

Thanks! ✌️
